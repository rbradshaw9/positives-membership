# Beta Communications Cadence

## Goal

Make beta testers feel guided and appreciated without overwhelming them.

## Recommended Cadence

### 1. Invitation

Send once when inviting the tester.

Include:

- why they were invited
- what kind of beta this is
- how to get in
- what kind of feedback is helpful
- any pricing / comp / grandfathering context

### 2. Welcome / First-Day Message

Send the same day they activate or immediately after they join.

Include:

- what to do first
- where to report issues
- reassurance that imperfect experiences are expected in beta

### 3. First Check-In

Send 3 to 5 days after activation.

Ask:

- what felt smooth
- what felt confusing
- whether they hit any blockers

### 4. Weekly Beta Update

Send once per week during active beta.

Include:

- what improved this week
- what we are still watching
- one or two specific things we want testers to try next

### 5. Closeout / Reflection

Send near the end of the beta phase.

Ask:

- what they valued most
- what they would improve first
- whether they want to continue as a paying or grandfathered member

## Tone

- warm
- grateful
- concise
- transparent

## Operational Notes

- keep the number of asks small
- do not send multiple overlapping check-ins in the same week
- if a tester reports a blocker, respond personally instead of waiting for the next cadence touch
