# Monday Keynote Deck Spec

## Goal
Create a polished `Keynote` presentation for the Monday discussion with Dr. Paul.

This should feel:
- spacious
- premium
- calm
- confident
- clear

The closest reference is an `Apple-style strategy deck`, but adapted to the Positives visual language:
- warmer
- softer
- more human
- less stark or corporate

This is not a reporting deck.
It is a `guided conversation deck`.

## Overall Direction
### Presentation job
The deck should do 3 things in order:
1. show what already exists
2. explain the transition from the old world to the new world
3. tee up the few decisions that matter most

### Slide count
- preferred: `12–16 slides`
- acceptable max: `20 slides`

### Visual approach
- full-bleed moments where helpful
- large type
- generous whitespace
- minimal text per slide
- one core point per slide
- subtle builds, fades, and clean transitions

### Positives visual system
- warm ivory / stone backgrounds
- deep charcoal text
- muted sage or moss green accents
- soft gradients or subtle atmospheric texture
- premium editorial composition

Avoid:
- clutter
- crowded bullet lists
- too many tables
- tiny text
- generic business-template slides

## Recommended Structure

### Slide 1 — Cover
**Title:** Positives Launch Planning

**Subtitle:** What we’ve built, what’s changing, and what we need to decide

**Footer:** Dr. Paul Jenkins + Ryan Bradshaw

**Visual:**
- strong, calm title slide
- subtle gradient or soft textured background
- no dense text

### Slide 2 — Where We Are Now
**Title:** We’ve built a real platform

**Content:**
- Positives is no longer just an idea
- core membership experience exists
- billing foundation exists
- admin system exists
- launch decisions are now mostly business and migration decisions

**Visual:**
- one strong statement slide
- maybe one product screenshot as supporting proof

### Slide 3 — What Already Exists
**Title:** What’s already built

**Content:**
- Stripe checkout and billing portal
- member-facing daily practice experience
- library, notes, and account area
- events and coaching surfaces
- admin content management
- affiliate/referral foundation
- public funnel and marketing pages

**Visual:**
- 6–7 elegant cards or icons
- very short labels

### Slide 4 — Product Proof
**Title:** The new member experience is real

**Content:**
- show 2–4 strong screenshots
- homepage / join / today / account or admin

**Visual:**
- screenshot collage with lots of breathing room
- minimal labels

### Slide 5 — Why This Matters
**Title:** This changes what’s possible

**Content:**
- new members can join through the new platform
- billing can move to Stripe
- content can be operated directly by the team
- old systems no longer have to define the future

**Visual:**
- big statement slide with short supporting lines

### Slide 6 — Current World Vs Future World
**Title:** From legacy stack to owned platform

**Left side:** Current world
- Infusionsoft / Keap
- Authorize.net
- ClickFunnels
- Liquid Web
- WordPress + plugin stack

**Right side:** Future world
- Stripe
- ActiveCampaign Pro
- Postmark
- Vercel
- Supabase
- FirstPromoter
- Vimeo

**Visual:**
- two clean columns with an arrow or progression

### Slide 7 — The Five Decisions
**Title:** What we need to decide today

**Content:**
1. what each membership tier should promise
2. whether Stripe is truly ready for live money
3. how we move existing members to Stripe
4. whether we approve the email-stack direction
5. how much of the old offer/funnel stack we really carry forward

**Visual:**
- 5 large numbered cards

### Slide 8 — Recommended Promise By Tier
**Title:** Keep the promise simple and truthful

**Content:**
- Membership
  - daily audio
  - weekly reflection
  - monthly theme
  - full core library
- Membership + Events
  - everything in Membership
  - live member events
  - replay access
- Coaching Circle
  - everything in Membership + Events
  - weekly live coaching
  - coaching replays

**Callout:**
- do not promise extra certified-coach support unless it is truly staffed

**Visual:**
- 3 elegant tier columns

### Slide 9 — Stripe Readiness
**Title:** Before we launch, Stripe must be real

**Content:**
- bank account and payouts
- business details
- verification and tax requirements
- right long-term owner
- live readiness, not just sandbox readiness

**Visual:**
- one clean checklist slide

### Slide 10 — Member Migration Recommendation
**Title:** Move existing members calmly, not all at once

**Content:**
- new members go to Stripe now
- existing members move in phases
- start with an announcement phase
- try payment-data migration first
- use guided payment-update flows only if needed
- do not shut down the old system too early

**Visual:**
- simple left-to-right path

### Slide 11 — Pricing Transition
**Title:** Simplify pricing over time

**Content:**
- current legacy cohorts include prices like `67`, `47`, and `07`
- recommended direction:
  - move legacy cohorts toward the current `37` plan
  - use exceptions only when intentionally approved

**Visual:**
- simple progression visual:
  - old prices -> transition -> 37 plan

### Slide 12 — Rebilling In Waves
**Title:** Phased rebilling protects revenue

**Content:**
1. new members already on Stripe
2. simple monthly members
3. legacy monthly cohorts
4. annual members
5. special cases and edge cases

**Rule:**
- do not move the next wave until the last one is stable

**Visual:**
- stepped wave diagram or horizontal lane diagram

### Slide 13 — Email Recommendation
**Title:** A cleaner email system

**Content:**
- App -> ActiveCampaign Pro -> Postmark
- ActiveCampaign becomes the automation brain
- Postmark becomes the transactional delivery layer
- essential account emails still send
- marketing emails stay unsubscribable

**Visual:**
- simple 3-box architecture

### Slide 14 — What We Rebuild First
**Title:** We do not need to move everything

**Content:**
- move now:
  - membership-critical experience
  - highest-value supporting offers
  - priority funnels
- later:
  - long-tail courses
  - old campaigns
  - lower-value legacy funnel sprawl

**Visual:**
- Now / Later / Maybe-never layout

### Slide 15 — Cost Story
**Title:** The overlap is temporary. The simplification is the point.

**Content:**
- target Positives stack
- legacy stack we want to retire
- overlap period costs more for a while
- long-term goal is a cleaner, lower-friction system

**Visual:**
- one elegant comparison table or stacked bar

**Numbers to include lightly:**
- ActiveCampaign Pro
- Postmark
- Keap
- ClickFunnels
- Liquid Web
- Vimeo

Do not overwhelm this slide.

### Slide 16 — What We Want To Leave With
**Title:** The outcome we want today

**Content:**
- approved membership promise
- clear Stripe readiness path
- phased rebilling approved
- ActiveCampaign Pro + Postmark approved
- membership-first migration scope approved
- owners for follow-up

**Visual:**
- clean closeout slide

## Animation Guidance
Keep animation minimal and polished.

Recommended:
- fade in by section
- dissolve transitions
- simple builds for 3–5 key points

Avoid:
- bounce
- dramatic zooms
- anything playful or distracting

## Typography And Layout
### Typography
- large headlines
- short supporting copy
- avoid dense paragraphs on slides

### Layout rhythm
Alternate between:
- statement slide
- structured decision slide
- visual comparison slide
- screenshot slide

This helps the deck feel more cinematic and less repetitive.

## Presenter Guidance
The presenter should:
- start with confidence by showing what exists
- keep the middle focused on the transition from old to new
- use the final third of the deck to get clear decisions

Do not narrate every detail on the slide.
Use the slides to support the conversation, not replace it.
